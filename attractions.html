Attractions

Find fun things to do during your trip here! All of these attractions can be found on the maps page!

Lincoln Memorial

A 19-ft. tall marble monument of the 16th President of the United States, Abraham Lincoln. It was built on May 30th, 1922, and at it's dedication, Lincoln's 78 year old son was present. It was registered as a historic place on the National Register of Historic Places, which happened on October 15th, 1966. The monument is very popular, with around 6 million people visting it every year. It is open 24 hours a day.

Washington Monument

A towering obelisk honoring the United States's first President, George Washington. At 555 feet tall, it is the tallest predominantly stone obelisk in the world. The dedication of this monument was on February 21, 1885, but the construction was not completed until 1888. Roughly 800,000 people visit it each and every year. It opens at 9am and closes at 10pm everyday of the week.

White House

Residence and workplace of the President of the United States. The building finished construction on November 1, 1800. It has undergone multiple restorations throughout its lifetime, but it is still one of the most important buildings in the United States. Tours of the White House are free of charge. To tour the White House a request must be sent through one's Member of Congress. More information on White House tours can be found here.

United States Capitol

Home of the United States Congress. Located on Capitol Hill. The building has over 16 acres of floor space! Construction was completed in 1800. Thomas Jefferson insisted that the legislative building be called "Capitol" rather than "Congress House". Tours of the building are free of charge. About 3-5 milion people visit every year. Tours are run Monday to Saturday from 8:50am to 3:20pm. More information on tours can be found here.

Smithsonian Institution

Admission free complex. The institution was established on August 10, 1846 for the purpose of increasing and defusing knowledge to everyone. Has a total holding of 154 million items. Contains 19 museums, a zoo, and nine research centers. The National Zoo is open from 9am to 4pm or 6pm in the summer. The majority of museums and galleries are open from 10am to 5:30pm daily. More information on hours of operation can be found here.

Thomas Jefferson Memorial

Memorial dedicated to Thomas Jefferson, the writer of the Declaration of Independence and one of the most important founding fathers of the United States. The monument was designed by John Russell Pope. Construction began in 1939 and it was completed in 1943. But it wasn't until 1947 that the bronze statue of Jefferson was added to the monument. Open 24 hours a day.

Arlington National Cemetary

A very large military cemetary. From the Civil War onward, many of the nation's fallen have been buried here. The cemetary has a total land area of 624 acres. It is controled by the United States Department of the Army. The cemetary was first created after the Civil War. Opens at 8am and closes at 5pm.

National Air and Space Museum

This museum documents the history of space exploration and aviation. The museum saw approximately 7.5 million visitors in 2016. Making this museum the third most visited in the world. It has several vehicles on display. Opens at 10am and closes at 5:30pm.
